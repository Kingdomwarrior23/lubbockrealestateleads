# How to Find a Top Real Estate Agent in Lubbock TX

The Lubbock Association of REALTORS has over 1,700 active members. That's a wide range of experience, specialty, and local knowledge. Here's how to filter for the ones who will actually get you results.

## What Makes a Lubbock REALTOR Actually Good

The Lubbock market is locally specific in ways that national platforms don't capture. Zip-code-level days-on-market data, which neighborhoods are seeing price reductions, where new construction is pricing out resale — these are things a REALTOR who's active in the Lubbock MLS knows and a Zillow algorithm doesn't.

The most important single credential: **recent transaction history in the specific area you're buying or selling.** An agent who specializes in southwest Lubbock isn't necessarily the right person to help you sell a Tech Terrace bungalow.

## Questions to Ask Before Hiring a Lubbock REALTOR

**For buyers:**
- "How many buyers have you represented in the last 12 months, and in which zip codes?"
- "What's your access to off-market and pre-market listings?"
- "How do you handle situations where you're competing with multiple offers?"
- "What does your buyer's agency agreement cover, and what are your compensation terms?" (Post-2024 NAR settlement, this must be disclosed upfront.)

**For sellers:**
- "What's the average days on market for homes you've listed in the last 12 months?"
- "What's your list-to-sale price ratio?"
- "How do you price a home — what data sources do you use?"
- "What's your marketing plan beyond MLS listing?"

## Red Flags When Interviewing Lubbock Agents

**Vague answers about market data.** A top agent should be able to give you specific numbers: "In 79423, homes under $250K are averaging 28 days on market right now." If they can only offer general impressions, their market knowledge is shallow.

**No recent transactions in your area.** An agent who hasn't sold a home in your target neighborhood in the last year doesn't have current, specific data to work from.

**Reluctance to explain compensation.** Post-2024 NAR settlement, all compensation terms must be transparent and negotiated. Any agent who's vague about what they're earning and why is a red flag.

**Overvaluing your home to win the listing.** This is called "buying the listing" — an agent quotes an inflated price to get you to sign, then pressures you for reductions after it sits. Check their list-to-sale price ratio to protect against this.

## What the Best Lubbock Agents Do Differently

Top agents in the Lubbock market typically have:
- Direct MLS access updated in real time (not 24-hour delayed feeds)
- Relationships with other local agents that generate off-market leads
- Specific neighborhood knowledge — they can name which blocks in 79423 are near flood-prone drainage channels, or which Tech Terrace streets have the highest rental turnover
- Systems for managing the paperwork and timelines that protect your earnest money and contingency deadlines

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I find a good real estate agent in Lubbock TX?**
Look for a Lubbock Association of REALTORS member with recent transaction history in your target neighborhood. Ask for MLS-verified sales data from the last 12 months. A top agent knows days-on-market trends by zip code.

**What percentage do real estate agents charge in Lubbock TX?**
Traditional commissions ranged 5–6% split between agents. Following the 2024 NAR settlement, buyer's agent compensation is negotiated separately. Ask for full transparency upfront.

**Should I use a local Lubbock REALTOR or a national platform?**
Local, every time. A Lubbock REALTOR with active MLS access has neighborhood-specific data national platforms don't — price reduction history, off-market listings, and days-on-market by zip code.
