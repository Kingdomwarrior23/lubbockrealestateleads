Common Questions from Lubbock Buyers & Sellers

The Lubbock Association of REALTORS reports a median of $235,000 as of March 2026 — up 6.6% year-over-year from January 2024. Entry-level homes under $200K represent 43.1% of all sales volume.
At 3.9 months of inventory (up 15% year-over-year), Lubbock is technically still a seller's market but trending toward balance. The $200K–$300K range has more room to negotiate than it has in 2–3 years. Entry-level under $200K remains competitive.
Southwest Lubbock (79423) leads in new construction and appreciation. Tech Terrace near Texas Tech has the best rental demand. The TTUHSC medical district is gaining momentum. South Lubbock (79412/79413) offers the best value per square foot for budget-conscious buyers.
Lubbock homes averaged 71 days on market in January 2025, up 18 days from January 2024. Well-priced entry-level homes move in 2–4 weeks. Overpriced homes in any range are sitting longer as inventory rises.
The 2024 NAR settlement changed how buyer's agent compensation works. Buyer's agents must now have a signed buyer representation agreement before touring homes, and their compensation must be disclosed and negotiated separately. Any agent we connect you with explains all fees upfront before you sign anything.
